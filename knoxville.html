The Knoxville Index

Knoxville has a ton of neat stuff! This page is a list I’m curating of a very small portion of it.

One of my goals has been to get more involved in local community and to prefer buying from small, local businesses. While there’s a ton of opportunities for that, a challenge for me has been finding and keeping track of them. I’m maintaining this index both for myself and anyone else who might find it useful - I’m trying to collect info and links to various community and advocacy groups, local businesses, city government info, and other places to find information and events in town.

I will eventually set up an RSS feed for this page that will list updates as I make them, so if you’re an RSS kind of person, stay tuned.

Local Community/Advocacy Groups

YES! Knoxville
Non-profit movement advocating for better housing, transportation, and community spaces. They usually have a monthly social meetup every first Sunday at a brewery.

JusticeKnox
Very large local non-profit action group. They are a church oriented organization, but you’re able to join without being affiliated with one. (That said, I emailed them once and never heard back, will try again soon.) The idea I think is that collectively the group decides what issues they want to consider/research/propose action on, and then collectively work towards engaging lawmakers/taking action, etc. They decide on one issue a year to focus on, and have a large initial meeting about it open to anyone. Upcoming “Nehemiah Action Assembly” for this year is April 8, 2025, 7-8:30 PM, Knoxville Civic Auditorium.

Knoxville Sustainable Future Center
Permaculture and sustainability group in south knox, haven’t been yet, but it looks like they have a newsletter and you can volunteer/join. They seem to have a pretty active calendar with classes and events (NOTE: calendar currently appears to be down)

Bike Walk Knoxville
Advocacy group for safer/accessible streets for biking and walking. I think they’re the organizers of the Walk with Elected Officials events, where the mayor and transportation board people come and anyone can join in a walk around Knoxville to discuss status, issues, etc.

Keep Knoxville Beautiful
Organization that regularly holds community litter pickup events and other neat volunteer opportunities. Next community cleanup is Saturday March 8, 2025, 9-12 over in North Knoxville

Trees Knoxville
Nonprofit working to improve Knovxille’s urban forest, they work with Keep Knoxville Beautiful and others and hold volunteer community events to come help plant trees. They offer classes for becoming a Volunteer Forester! Next session registration opens in April 2025.

SEEED
Socially Equal Energy Efficient Development, non-profit geared towards helping train young adults in sustainability related careers and building regenerative communitites. They have opportunities for volunteering, and have projects ranging from food forests to building better housing. UT-Batelle apparently partners with SEEED!

YPK - Young Professionals of Knoxville
Social and professional development group, meant to foster connections between peers, local businesses, community, etc. First time membership is $200, $100 per year after that.

Hardin Valley Business & Community Alliance
Local community group, membership is $50 a year, looks like they meet every third wednesday of the month. I think they get involved in various volunteer projects, their website highlighted an event where they planted some trees around Solway. First two meetings for new people are free.

Knoxville Democrats
Knox county democratic party. Self explanatory :D

Knox County Community Gardens & Growers Alliance
Community gardens, volunteer opportunities, looks like you can become a member and also get access to their educational resources etc.

Knoxville Regenerative Co-op (FB group link)
Permaculture, homesteading, and sustainable living group. They have a monthly social “Earth Talk” every 3rd Wednesday, 6-9 at Southside Garage in SoKno.

Knoxville Housing Cooperative
11 units in 4th and gill neighborhood. A housing co-op is community owned housing/no external landlord. I don’t pretend to understand how equity works with this, but it’s a thing and they have a good about page!

CAC Beardsley Community Farm
Community farm near downtown where you can volunteer, take classes, and is also a CSA.

Nourish Knoxville
Nonprofit that manages the local farmers markets. They have volunteer opportunities, and have maps/info on all of the stands for upcoming markets.

Knoxville Official Info Things

knoxvilletn.gov
Official city of Knoxville government website.

I BIKE KNX
Knoxville Regional Bicycle Program, part of the local transportation planning organization. They have resources (map of all racks, facilities, and bikeability status of roads in knoxville), information on ways to volunteer around town for biking related things etc.

KAT Bus
Site for our local bus system. Busses around here are $1 per ride, and you can get a KAT Pay card or use KAT Pay mobile which will automatically turn 2 rides in a day into a day pass, and 30 paid rides in a month into a month pass (essentially you never pay more than $2 a day, and no more than $30 a month.) Planning routes and live tracking where next bus on a route is can be done either directly on the website or through the Transit app. Both google maps and magic earth are decent at correctly planning routes via busses.

Knoxville/Knox County Planning
City planning, various documents about long term plans, zoning, projects etc can be found here. They also have a calendar for when their public meetings are.

Knoxville Transportation Planning Organization
They help planning and advising other government agencies for better public transportation and better/safer walkability and bikability of Knoxville and nearby regions. Tons of information and documents for various plans and local information. This org is responsible for I BIKE KNX and Smart Trips.

Knox county election commision
General information and resources for local elections.

KGIS
Extremely detailed GIS map of every possible map thing you could want to know map-wise - includes things such as zoning, political districts, down to even what type of soil is present in what areas.

Unofficial Info Things

Pick Tennessee Products
Information and resources on local agriculture etc. Has links for local CSAs, farmers markets everywhere across the state, etc.

LocalHarvest
Similar to above, information on farms, CSAs, and markets around here.

Stores and Local Businesses

KnoxFill
Awesome zero waste/refill household goods! Bring your own jars or pick up some there.

Persian galleries
Handcrafted traditional rugs!

Habitat for Humanity ReStore
Proceeds go towards helping housing for local families, they sell new/gently used furniture, appliances, decor, household items, etc.

River Sports Outfitters
Local independent outdoors-y store on Sutherland! They also have a climbing gym attached!

Outdoor Gear Revival
Secondhand outdoorsy stuff store.

Loopville Yarn
Yarn/knitting/crocheting store, they have all sorts of yarn crafting related things and classes!

Spice & Tea Exchange
Technically a franchise, but it really is an awesome store downtown, they have a wall of tea jars you can smell, a ton of spices and salts, and you can try a cup of any of their teas in store.

Groceries

Three Rivers Market
Local food co-op! Lots of bulk goods, spices, and teas, and you can bring in your own containers to refill.

Sunrise Supermarket
Asian grocery store on Kingston pike, this is where you go if you want more than just the two varieties of lentils you can find at Kroger.

Market Wagon
It’s like grubhub, but produce and products from nearby farms instead! It’s an “online farmer’s market”, where you place your order, and once a week those orders are collected at a hub and delivered to you.

Janta Farmers Market (yelp link)
Indian grocery store right next to Sunrise, haven’t been yet but I’ve heard good things on the Knoxville subreddit and I want to go check it out!

Pratts Country Store (FB link)
Little local market over on Tazewell pike, my mum and I used to go here a lot when I was growing up!

Cruze Farm Dairy
Not really a grocery store, but they sell milk at their ice cream shop downtown and it’s DELICIOUS.

Farm Fresh Produce (yelp link)
Local produce and goods market on Sutherland, haven’t been yet but I intend to! According to this reddit comment they sell milk in returnable bottles. I’ll update this when I go and determine if this is in fact still the case!

Butler & Bailey Market
Local grocery store over on Northshore. (Haven’t been.)

Farm South (FB link)
Marketplace in south knox for local farms and goods, haven’t been yet.

Thrift stores

There are a lot of KARM thrift stores around, but they have a bit of a reputation for questionable ethics and way overpricing everything. Some other thrift stores around:

  • Greater Goods Thrift (Kingston pike) - they sell clothes by the pound, outdated items move from Planet Xchange here. Women and LGBTQ+ owned!
  • Planet Xchange (Kingston pike)
  • Amvets (West knox)
  • “The Thrift Store” (Kingston pike)
  • Ladies of Charity (near 4th and Gill)
  • Community Chest (near 4th and Gill)

See this reddit post with even more. (I will at some point move some more over here.)

Classes

Woodcraft
Various woodworking classes.

Mighty Mud
Pottery/throwing, in south knox.

Relay Ridge
Various artistry type classes etc.

PSTCC Lifelong Learning
Pellissippi offers all sorts of courses that anyone can take, from music, painting, cooking, stained glass, they have a pretty extensive catalog each semester. They don’t have an easy to find course catalog easily accessible from their website, but here’s the current Spring 2025 catalog

IJAMS
IJAMS has a ton of neat events and class-type things you can go to! Highly recommend their evening/night paddles on the river when they’re offered, it’s a ton of fun!
